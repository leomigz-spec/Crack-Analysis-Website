Crack Analysis Measuring Device

For Structural Integrity of Buildings Using Peridynamics Model

Overview Problem Objectives Methodology Results Conclusion

Project Overview

This research focuses on developing a crack analysis measuring device to assess the structural integrity of buildings. The system utilizes a peridynamics-based model to analyze crack propagation and structural damage.

Problem Statement

Manual crack inspection in buildings is often subjective, time-consuming, and prone to human error. Undetected crack propagation can lead to severe structural failure and safety risks.

Research Objectives

  • To design a crack analysis measuring device for buildings
  • To evaluate crack behavior using the peridynamics model
  • To improve early detection of structural damage

Methodology

The proposed system captures crack measurements using sensors and applies peridynamics modeling to analyze stress distribution and crack propagation.

Expected Results

The device is expected to provide accurate crack measurements and reliable structural integrity assessments, supporting early maintenance and safety decisions.

Conclusion

This research aims to contribute to structural engineering by combining advanced modeling techniques with practical crack measurement technology.
